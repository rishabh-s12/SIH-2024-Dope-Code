ANTI DOPING CASES
IN THE PAST AND RECENT YEARS

Ben Johnson (1988)

Ben Johnson, a Canadian sprinter, won the 100 meters gold medal at the 1988 Seoul Olympics with a record time of 9.79 seconds. However, just days later, he tested positive for stanozolol, an anabolic steroid.

As a result, he was stripped of his gold medal, and the title was awarded to Carl Lewis, the American sprinter who finished second. Johnson's case became one of the most notorious doping scandals in Olympic history.

Anti-doping cases in the past

  • Definition: Anti-doping cases involve incidents where athletes are found to have violated anti-doping regulations by using prohibited substances or methods to enhance performance.
  • Conclusion of such cases:The detection of such violations can result in sanctions, including fines, suspensions, or permanent bans from competition. They can have significant implications for athletes' careers, reputations, and the integrity of the sport.

Lance Armstrong (1999-2005)

Lance Armstrong, an American cyclist, was a seven-time Tour de France winner. However, in 2012, an investigation by the United States Anti-Doping Agency (USADA) revealed that Armstrong had used performance-enhancing drugs throughout his career. Armstrong eventually admitted to doping in a televised interview with Oprah Winfrey in 2013. As a result, he was stripped of all his Tour de France titles and banned from professional cycling for life.

Marion Jones (2000)

  • Marion Jones, an American track and field athlete, won five medals (three gold and two bronze) at the 2000 Sydney Olympics. In 2007, Jones admitted to using steroids before the Olympics as part of the BALCO scandal. She was subsequently stripped of her Olympic medals, and her career was tarnished by the scandal. Jones also served a six-month prison sentence for lying to federal investigators.

Maria Sharapova (2016)

Russian tennis star Maria Sharapova tested positive for meldonium, a drug that had been added to the World Anti-Doping Agency's (WADA) banned list at the start of 2016. Sharapova admitted to taking the drug for health reasons but claimed she was unaware it had been banned. Initially suspended for two years, her ban was later reduced to 15 months following an appeal. She returned to professional tennis in April 2017.

Shane Warne (2003)

Australian cricketer Shane Warne, one of the greatest leg-spin bowlers in cricket history, tested positive for a banned diuretic just before the 2003 Cricket World Cup. Warne claimed that the drug was given to him by his mother to help him lose weight, not to enhance performance. Despite this defense, he was banned from cricket for 12 months by the Australian Cricket Board (ACB). Warne returned to cricket after his ban and continued his successful career.

Shelby Houlihan (2021)

American middle-distance runner Shelby Houlihan tested positive for the anabolic steroid nandrolone in 2021. Houlihan claimed that the positive test resulted from consuming a contaminated burrito.

Anti Doping Cases in Recent Years

  • Salwa Eid Naser (2020)

  • Bahraini sprinter Salwa Eid Naser, the 2019 World Champion in the 400m, was provisionally suspended in 2020 for failing to comply with anti-doping whereabouts requirements. Naser missed multiple drug tests in a 12-month period, leading to her suspension.

    Naser's case was scrutinized by the Athletics Integrity Unit (AIU). Although she was initially cleared by a disciplinary tribunal, the World Anti-Doping Agency (WADA) appealed, and in 2021 she received a two-year ban.

Sun Yang (2020)

Chinese swimmer Sun Yang, a three-time Olympic gold medalist, was found guilty of violating anti-doping rules after he refused to provide a blood sample during a surprise doping test in 2018. Sun allegedly destroyed the blood samples with a hammer.

Sun Yang was initially handed an eight-year ban by the Court of Arbitration for Sport (CAS) in 2020. However, the ban was later reduced to four years and three months upon appeal in 2021

Andrea Iannone (2019)

  • Italian MotoGP rider Andrea Iannone tested positive for the anabolic steroid drostanolone during a routine drug test in 2019.
  • Iannone was initially banned for 18 months, but the CAS later extended the ban to four years after an appeal by WADA, effectively ending his racing career.

Therese Johaug (2016)

Norwegian cross-country skier Therese Johaug tested positive for the banned substance clostebol, a steroid found in a lip balm she claimed to have used unknowingly.

Johaug received a 13-month suspension, causing her to miss the 2017 World Championships but allowing her to return for the 2018 Winter Olympics, where she won multiple medals.
